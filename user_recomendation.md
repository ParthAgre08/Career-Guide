**Career Roadmap for a Data Scientist**

Based on the student's preference description, academic strengths, and personality scores, I recommend a career roadmap that aligns with their interests and abilities.

**Suitable Career Options:**
1. **Data Analyst**: Responsible for collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to help organizations make informed decisions.
2. **Business Intelligence Developer**: Creates data visualizations and reports to support business strategy and decision-making.
3. **Machine Learning Engineer**: Develops and deploys machine learning models to solve complex problems in various industries.

**Required Degree:**
A Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or a related field is recommended.

**Entrance Exams:**
1. **Graduate Record Examination (GRE)**: A standardized test that measures verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, and analytical writing skills.
2. **Linear Algebra and Calculus**: Proficiency in these subjects is essential for data science careers.

**3-Year Roadmap:**

Year 1:

* Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or a related field
* Take courses in linear algebra, calculus, statistics, and programming languages (Python, R, SQL)
* Participate in data science competitions, hackathons, or projects to gain practical experience

Year 2:

* Continue taking courses in machine learning, data visualization, and big data analytics
* Develop a personal project or contribute to open-source projects on platforms like Kaggle or GitHub
* Gain experience with data analysis tools and software (Tableau, Power BI, Python libraries)

Year 3:

* Take advanced courses in deep learning, natural language processing, or computer vision
* Participate in internships or research projects to gain hands-on experience
* Prepare for entrance exams (GRE) and apply to graduate programs or data science certifications

**Required Skills:**

1. **Programming languages**: Python, R, SQL, Julia
2. **Data analysis tools**: Tableau, Power BI, Python libraries (NumPy, pandas, scikit-learn)
3. **Machine learning frameworks**: TensorFlow, PyTorch, Keras
4. **Statistics and mathematics**: Linear algebra, calculus, probability, statistics
5. **Communication skills**: Present data insights effectively to non-technical stakeholders

**Practical Advice:**

1. **Stay updated with industry trends**: Follow data science blogs, attend conferences, or participate in online forums.
2. **Build a personal project portfolio**: Showcase your projects on platforms like GitHub or Kaggle.
3. **Network with professionals**: Join data science communities, attend meetups, or connect with mentors.
4. **Practice regularly**: Set aside time to work on personal projects or contribute to open-source projects.
5. **Be prepared for continuous learning**: Data science is a constantly evolving field, so be ready to adapt and learn new skills.

By following this career roadmap, the student can transition into a successful data scientist career, leveraging their academic strengths, personality scores, and preference description.
